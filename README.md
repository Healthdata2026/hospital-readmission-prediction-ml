🏥 Background & Importance
Hospital readmissions—especially within 30 days of discharge—are a major challenge for healthcare systems worldwide. They are often associated with increased healthcare costs, reduced quality of care, and poor patient outcomes. In the United States, unplanned readmissions are closely monitored under programs like the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services Hospital Readmissions Reduction Program, which penalizes hospitals for higher-than-expected readmission rates.
Many readmissions are preventable and are influenced by factors such as inadequate discharge planning, comorbid conditions, medication adherence, and lack of follow-up care. However, identifying high-risk patients in advance remains difficult using traditional rule-based approaches.
This is where machine learning can add significant value. By analyzing large volumes of patient-level data—including demographics, clinical history, and hospital utilization patterns—predictive models can help:
•	Identify patients at high risk of readmission
•	Enable early intervention and personalized care plans
•	Improve hospital resource allocation
•	Reduce avoidable healthcare costs
This project aims to leverage machine learning techniques to build a predictive model for 30-day hospital readmissions, providing actionable insights that support data-driven decision-making in healthcare.
